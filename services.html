Blood Test Laboratory Services

Test Name Description Normal Range
Complete Blood Count (CBC) A blood test that measures different components of the blood. WBC: 4,000-11,000 cells/μL
Hb: 12-16 g/dL
Platelet: 150,000-450,000 cells/μL
Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP) Measures glucose, calcium, and other electrolyte levels in the blood. Glucose: 70-100 mg/dL
Calcium: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
Sodium: 135-145 mEq/L
Lipid Profile Measures cholesterol levels in the blood. Total Cholesterol: 200 mg/dL
HDL Cholesterol: >40 mg/dL (men), >50 mg/dL (women)
Liver Function Tests (LFTs) Measures liver enzymes and other substances in the blood. ALT (SGPT): 7-56 U/L
AST (SGOT): 5-40 U/L
Total Bilirubin: 0.1-1.2 mg/dL
Thyroid Function Panel Measures thyroid hormone levels in the blood. TSH: 0.4-4.0 mIU/L
Free T4: 0.8-1.8 ng/dL
Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP) Provides information about kidney and liver function, glucose, calcium, and electrolytes. Glucose: 70-100 mg/dL
Calcium: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
BUN: 7-20 mg/dL
Creatinine: 0.6-1.3 mg/dL
ALT (SGPT): 7-56 U/L
Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c) Measures long-term blood sugar control. Less than 5.7%
Prothrombin Time (PT) Measures how long it takes for blood to clot. 11-13.5 seconds
Partial Thromboplastin Time (PTT) Assesses the blood's ability to clot. 25-35 seconds
Urinalysis Examines urine for signs of disease or infection. Varies by parameter (e.g., pH: 4.6-8.0)
